Keeping your children safe in the digital world

Online Safety for Children

Teaching children about online safety is essential in today's digital age. This guide provides practical advice for parents to help protect their children online.

Age-Appropriate Guidelines

  • Ages 5-7: Supervised access, educational content only, no social media
  • Ages 8-10: Limited supervised access, introduce basic privacy concepts
  • Ages 11-13: Guided independence, discuss digital footprint and online reputation
  • Ages 14+: More independence with ongoing communication about risks

Essential Parental Controls

  • Content filters: Block inappropriate websites and content
  • Screen time limits: Set daily usage limits for devices and apps
  • App restrictions: Control which apps can be downloaded
  • Location tracking: Know where your child's device is located
  • Activity monitoring: Review browsing history and app usage
  • Purchase controls: Require approval for in-app purchases

Teaching Digital Citizenship

  • Respect others: Teach kindness and empathy online
  • Protect privacy: Never share personal information with strangers
  • Think before posting: Understand that online content is permanent
  • Recognize cyberbullying: Know when to seek help
  • Critical thinking: Question information and verify sources
  • Balance screen time: Encourage offline activities

Warning Signs to Watch For

  • Quickly switching screens when you approach
  • Becoming withdrawn or secretive about online activities
  • Excessive time spent online, especially at night
  • Emotional changes after using devices
  • Unexpected friend requests or messages from strangers
  • New accounts on platforms you didn't approve

Talking to Your Kids About Online Safety

  • Start conversations early and keep them ongoing
  • Create a judgment-free environment for discussion
  • Use real-world examples and news stories
  • Explain the "why" behind safety rules
  • Listen to their concerns and experiences
  • Set a good example with your own device use

Family Technology Agreement

Consider creating a family technology agreement that includes:

  • Designated screen-free times (meals, bedtime)
  • Device-free zones (bedrooms, dining room)
  • Rules about sharing passwords (with parents only)
  • Guidelines for social media use
  • Consequences for breaking rules
  • Regular check-ins and updates to rules

Cyberbullying Prevention

  • Teach children not to respond to bullying messages
  • Save evidence (screenshots) of bullying
  • Block and report bullies on platforms
  • Notify school officials if classmates are involved
  • Consider contacting authorities for threats
  • Provide emotional support to victims
