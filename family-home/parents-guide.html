Protect your children in the digital world

Why Digital Parenting Matters

Today's children are digital natives, growing up in a world where technology is everywhere. As parents, it's our responsibility to guide them safely through the digital landscape while fostering healthy technology habits.

üí° Remember: The goal isn't to scare children away from technology, but to teach them how to use it safely and responsibly.

Age-Appropriate Guidelines

Ages 5-8: Early Digital Exploration

  • Keep devices in common areas of the home
  • Use kid-friendly browsers and search engines
  • Set strict content filters
  • Limit screen time to 1-2 hours per day
  • Always supervise online activities
  • Teach about not sharing personal information

Ages 9-12: Growing Independence

  • Introduce supervised social media (with parental accounts)
  • Teach about cyberbullying and how to respond
  • Discuss appropriate vs. inappropriate content
  • Set clear rules about online communication
  • Review browser history regularly
  • Teach critical thinking about online information

Ages 13-17: Teens Online

  • Balance privacy with safety monitoring
  • Discuss digital footprint and online reputation
  • Address sexting and inappropriate content sharing
  • Teach about online predators and grooming tactics
  • Set boundaries for device usage (no phones at dinner, in bedrooms after bedtime)
  • Encourage open communication about online experiences

Parental Control Tools

Built-in Platform Controls

  • Apple Screen Time: iOS/macOS parental controls
  • Google Family Link: Android device management
  • Microsoft Family Safety: Windows and Xbox controls
  • Amazon FreeTime: Kindle and Fire tablet controls

Third-Party Solutions

  • Net Nanny: Comprehensive filtering and monitoring
  • Qustodio: Cross-platform parental control
  • Bark: Social media monitoring and alerts
  • Circle Home Plus: Network-level filtering

Common Online Dangers

Cyberbullying

  • Watch for signs: withdrawn behavior, reluctance to use devices, emotional distress
  • Teach children to save evidence (screenshots)
  • Block and report bullies on platforms
  • Contact school administrators if necessary
  • Consider involving law enforcement for serious threats

Online Predators

  • Teach children never to meet online friends in person
  • Warn about adults asking for personal information
  • Monitor gaming platforms and chat features
  • Discuss grooming tactics (excessive flattery, secrecy requests)
  • Keep communication lines open so children feel safe reporting concerns

Inappropriate Content

  • Use content filters on all devices
  • Set safe search settings on search engines
  • Monitor YouTube and video streaming services
  • Discuss what to do if they encounter inappropriate content

Privacy Risks

  • Teach children never to share: full name, address, school, phone number, photos
  • Review privacy settings on apps and games together
  • Explain why location sharing can be dangerous
  • Discuss the permanence of online posts

Creating a Family Media Plan

Set Clear Rules

  • Device-free zones (dinner table, bedrooms)
  • Screen time limits by age
  • Content guidelines
  • Social media age requirements
  • Consequences for breaking rules

Lead by Example

  • Follow the same rules you set for your children
  • Put your phone away during family time
  • Demonstrate healthy technology habits
  • Share your own positive online experiences
‚úì Family Contract: Create a written agreement that everyone signs, outlining technology rules and expectations.

Gaming Safety

  • Review game ratings (ESRB) before purchase
  • Disable or monitor in-game chat features
  • Set spending limits on game purchases
  • Play games with your children to understand them
  • Use console parental controls
  • Discuss appropriate online behavior in multiplayer games

Social Media Guidance

Before Allowing Social Media

  • Ensure your child meets the platform's minimum age requirement
  • Review privacy settings together
  • Set account to private
  • Approve followers/friends
  • Discuss appropriate content to post

Ongoing Monitoring

  • Follow or friend your children on social media
  • Periodically review their posts and interactions
  • Watch for signs of cyberbullying or harassment
  • Discuss concerning posts or behavior calmly

Teaching Digital Citizenship

Help children become responsible digital citizens:

  • Respect others online
  • Think before posting
  • Protect personal information
  • Recognize and report inappropriate content
  • Understand copyright and intellectual property
  • Practice empathy in digital communications
  • Stand up against cyberbullying
