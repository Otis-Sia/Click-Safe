Social Media Privacy: Share Smart

The Social Media Landscape

Social media platforms are integral to modern communication, but they come with privacy and security risks. Criminals use social media to gather personal information, impersonate users, and execute targeted attacks.

→ Learn more about Online Social Networks

Privacy Settings & Protection

Review Your Privacy Settings

Each platform has privacy controls. Take time to configure them:

  • Set your profile to private instead of public
  • Limit who can see your posts and personal information
  • Control who can tag you in photos/videos
  • Review and remove old, sensitive posts
  • Disable location sharing

Be Careful What You Share

  • Don't share your phone number, address, or birthdate publicly
  • Avoid posting about travel plans before you leave
  • Don't share photos of your children or family without permission
  • Be cautious about financial information
  • Avoid sharing details that could be used for password recovery questions

Recognize Phishing & Scams

Watch out for common social media threats:

  • Fake Links: Don't click links from unknown accounts
  • Fake Profiles: Verify accounts before trusting them
  • Quizzes & Games: These often harvest personal data
  • Too Good to Be True Offers: Free money, products, or jobs are usually scams
  • Urgent Messages: "Verify your account" messages are often phishing attempts

→ Learn more about phishing attacks

Secure Your Accounts

  • Use unique, strong passwords for each platform
  • Enable two-factor authentication on all social media accounts
  • Review connected apps and revoke unnecessary permissions
  • Check active sessions and log out from unfamiliar devices
  • Add a recovery email or phone number to your account

Friend & Connection Management

  • Only accept friend requests from people you know
  • Regularly review your friends/followers list
  • Remove connections that seem suspicious
  • Be cautious about accepting requests from anyone offering prizes or romance
  • Don't share sensitive information through private messages

Common Social Media Threats

Social networks are prime targets for various forms of cybercrime. Understanding these threats is the first step in protection.

Privacy Risks & Identity Theft

Privacy in OSNs: Privacy is control over your data. Default public settings and oversharing often blur these boundaries, allowing even private data to be inferred.

Identity Theft: Attackers use stolen names, photos, and birthdates to create fake profiles or commit fraud. This often increases with the growth of the platform.

→ Learn more about identity theft

Online Fraud & Impersonation

Trust between friends is often exploited for fraud:

  • Impersonation Scams: Criminals pose as friends in "emergencies" to ask for money.
  • Fake Profiles: Bots or bad actors create attractive or authoritative personas to gain trust.
  • Loan Scams: Predatory lending or fake financial aid offers targeting users.

Modern Threats

  • Cloning Attacks: Creating an identical copy of your profile to trick your friends.
  • Location Leakage: Check-ins and geotags revealing your physical location.
  • Clickjacking: Tricking you into clicking something different than what you perceive.
  • Deepfakes & AI-Generated Content: Realistic fake videos, audio, and images used for fraud and manipulation.
  • AI-Powered Misinformation: Automated bots spreading false information at massive scale.

→ Learn about deepfakes and AI threats

If Your Account Is Compromised

  1. Change your password immediately
  2. Review recent activity and remove unauthorized connections
  3. Check for and revoke access to connected apps
  4. Enable two-factor authentication
  5. Inform contacts that your account may have been compromised
  6. Report to the platform's support team
  7. Monitor for fraudulent activity

Quick Checklist

  • ☐ Review and tighten privacy settings on all platforms
  • ☐ Set profile to private
  • ☐ Use unique passwords for each platform
  • ☐ Enable two-factor authentication
  • ☐ Review connected apps and remove unnecessary ones
  • ☐ Be cautious about what you share
  • ☐ Recognize and report phishing attempts
