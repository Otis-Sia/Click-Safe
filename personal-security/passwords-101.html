Why Strong Passwords Matter

Your password is the key to your digital life. A weak password is like leaving your front door unlocked ‚Äì it's an open invitation for cybercriminals to access your personal information, financial accounts, and identity.

Creating Strong Passwords

The Golden Rules

  • Length matters: Use at least 12 characters (longer is better)
  • Mix it up: Combine uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and special characters
  • Avoid the obvious: No personal information, dictionary words, or common patterns
  • Unique for each account: Never reuse passwords across different sites
üí° Pro Tip: Use a passphrase! "Coffee!Beach@Sunrise2026" is easier to remember than "C0ff33#2026" and just as secure.

  • ‚ùå Personal information (names, birthdays, addresses)
  • ‚ùå Dictionary words
  • ‚ùå Sequential numbers or letters (12345, abcdef)
  • ‚ùå Common substitutions (Pa$$w0rd)
  • ‚ùå Keyboard patterns (qwerty, asdfgh)

Password Management

Use a Password Manager

Password managers are secure digital vaults that store all your passwords. They can:

  • Generate strong, unique passwords for each account
  • Automatically fill in login credentials
  • Sync across all your devices
  • Alert you about weak or compromised passwords
‚úì Recommended Options: 1Password, LastPass, Bitwarden, or your browser's built-in password manager

Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Add an extra layer of security by requiring a second form of verification:

  • SMS codes sent to your phone
  • Authentication apps (Google Authenticator, Authy)
  • Hardware security keys
  • Biometric verification (fingerprint, face ID)

Regular Password Maintenance

  • Review your passwords quarterly
  • Change passwords immediately if a breach is reported
  • Delete unused accounts
  • Update recovery information regularly

Quick Action Checklist

  1. Choose and install a password manager
  2. Update your most important passwords (email, banking, social media)
  3. Enable two-factor authentication wherever available
  4. Create a secure master password for your password manager
  5. Delete or update any duplicate passwords
