Take control of your digital footprint on social platforms

Understanding Social Media Privacy

Social media platforms are designed to encourage sharing, but oversharing can put your privacy and security at risk. Learning to control what you share and who can see it is essential for protecting yourself online.

‚ö†Ô∏è Remember: Once something is posted online, it can be difficult or impossible to completely remove it, even if you delete it later.

Privacy Settings Review

Facebook/Meta Platforms

  • Review who can see your posts (Public, Friends, Custom)
  • Limit who can send you friend requests
  • Control who can look you up using your email/phone number
  • Review and remove old posts using Activity Log
  • Limit past posts visibility
  • Disable facial recognition (if available in your region)
  • Review third-party app permissions

Instagram

  • Switch to a private account
  • Control who can comment on your posts
  • Disable activity status
  • Review and remove followers
  • Limit story sharing to close friends
  • Turn off location services for posts

Twitter/X

  • Protect your tweets (make account private)
  • Control who can tag you in photos
  • Limit who can see your email and phone number
  • Disable location information
  • Review connected apps and revoke unnecessary access

LinkedIn

  • Control who can see your connections
  • Limit profile visibility to connections only
  • Turn off activity broadcasts for updates
  • Control who can send you invitations
  • Review profile viewing options

TikTok

  • Set account to private
  • Control who can comment, duet, or stitch your videos
  • Disable location services
  • Review and adjust content preferences
  • Limit data collection in privacy settings

What Not to Share

Avoid posting information that could compromise your security:

  • ‚ùå Full birth date (year especially)
  • ‚ùå Current location or real-time check-ins
  • ‚ùå Home address or identifiable landmarks near home
  • ‚ùå Phone numbers
  • ‚ùå Vacation plans (wait until you're back)
  • ‚ùå Financial information
  • ‚ùå Children's full names, schools, or locations
  • ‚ùå Photos of credit cards, IDs, or documents
  • ‚ùå Your daily routine or schedule
üí° Think Before You Post: Ask yourself: "Could this information be used to steal my identity, break into my home, or harm me in any way?"

Photo and Video Security

Metadata Awareness

  • Photos can contain location data, timestamps, and device information
  • Strip metadata before sharing sensitive photos
  • Turn off geotagging on your phone's camera

Background Information

  • Check photo backgrounds for sensitive information
  • Avoid showing mail, packages, or documents with personal details
  • Be careful with reflections in mirrors or windows

Managing Your Digital Footprint

Regular Audits

  • Review your posts quarterly
  • Delete or archive old content that's no longer relevant
  • Search for yourself to see what's publicly visible
  • Use privacy checkup tools provided by platforms

Friend/Follower Management

  • Regularly review your friends/followers list
  • Remove people you don't know or no longer trust
  • Be selective about accepting new connection requests
  • Create custom friend lists for different sharing levels

Avoiding Social Media Scams

Common Scams to Watch For

  • Fake friend requests from accounts impersonating people you know
  • Quizzes and surveys designed to steal personal information
  • Too-good-to-be-true contests and giveaways
  • Messages with suspicious links
  • Romance scams and catfishing
‚ö†Ô∏è Red Flags: Requests for money, pressure to act quickly, messages with poor grammar, or anything that seems too good to be true.

Two-Factor Authentication

Enable 2FA on all your social media accounts:

  • Use an authenticator app rather than SMS when possible
  • Save backup codes in a secure location
  • Set up recovery methods in case you lose access
